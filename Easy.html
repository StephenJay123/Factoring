By Stephen Jay

Easy Trinomial: A Polynomial with 3 terms that has a leading coefficient of 1

Equation:

How To Solve:

The first step in factoring an Easy Trinomial is to factor out anything if possible.
This is done by taking out the Greatest Common Factor for every term and dividing each term by it.

The second step in factoring an Easy Trinomial is to find two numbers that both add into C and multiply into B.

The third step in factoring an Easy Trinomial is to put both of those numbers next to the variable in brackets.

The final step in factoring an Easy Trinomial is to make sure that you cannot factor any further.
If you can't factor any further, that is your final answer.
